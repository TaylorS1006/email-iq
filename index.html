    What this tells us

    The highest-performing emails in this dataset share two traits: they target small, already-engaged segments (registrants, attendees, or prospects who raised their hand) and they use urgency or exclusivity language tied to a specific, tangible moment—on-demand availability, a gift claim, or a missed connection. The weakest performers are broad cold or warm outreach blasts sent to 10,000+ contacts with generic event-discovery framing. The clearest takeaway is that segmentation does more work than subject line copy: the same 'Elevate' event family spans open rates from 64.6% down to 15.7%, almost entirely explained by audience size and proximity to the event moment. Next time, prioritize tighter segmentation and post-event follow-up sequences over optimizing wording on large cold sends.
